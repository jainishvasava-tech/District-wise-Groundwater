About the project

A comprehensive system for tracking and managing groundwater resources across Surat District. Built to curb illegal extraction and provide the visibility required for sustainable, data-driven decision-making.

The challenge

Textile and chemical industries in Surat rely heavily on water. To meet demand, illegal suppliers have emerged, extracting groundwater without regulation‚Äîreducing underground reserves and risking summer shortages.

Agencies lack reliable, monthly visibility into groundwater levels across jurisdictions. Without continuous monitoring, it‚Äôs hard to identify illegal activity or plan for water-scarce periods.

Limited visibility

Our solution

  • Automated monitoring: District-wide sensors and data ingestion for continuous tracking.
  • Monthly taluka-wise reports: Standardized summaries and charts for each taluka.
  • Real-time alerts: Identify suspicious extraction patterns to flag potential illegal activity.
  • Decision support: Insights help agencies plan allocation and interventions with confidence.
  • Predictive planning: Uses historical trends to forecast summer water needs and allocation requirements.
  • Data transparency: Public portal to create awareness and trust through accessible groundwater information.
Taluka-wise groundwater level chart (placeholder)

Key capabilities

  • Coverage: Surat District with taluka granularity.
  • Frequency: Monthly level snapshots with trend analysis.
  • Anomaly detection: Threshold-based flags and pattern deviation alerts.
  • Compliance support: Evidence packs to assist enforcement against illegal extraction.
  • Open data: Exportable CSV/JSON and public dashboards.

Implementation overview

  • Data pipeline: Sensor input ‚Üí validation ‚Üí storage ‚Üí analytics ‚Üí dashboards.
  • Reporting: Automated monthly generation per taluka with charts and commentary.
  • Alerting: Real-time thresholds and anomaly rules configurable by agencies.
  • Access control: Role-based views for agencies and public read-only access.

Get started

  • Request access: Enable agency credentials for dashboards and alert management.
  • Submit data sources: Register sensors and legacy datasets for ingestion.
  • Engage the public: Publish the transparency portal to raise awareness.
